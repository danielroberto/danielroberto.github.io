Context
I was assigned to support the user testing for a mobile app that allows the new audio concept to be used.
The design opportunity

Create a new audio format and feature for the future of music and the music listening experience.
The approach
Conduct user concept testing to gather quantitative data and qualitative feedback.
My role
Test moderator and technical support.
The process
Research
There has been much research and development for this feature / format. However, what we aimed to do is to get actual user feedback on their thoughts of it. We hired a 3rd party recruiting agency to recruit participants who meet our minimum requirements to fit the target demographic for such a feature.

Planning
The plan was to have 3 participants (previously 6 in a prior study) come in and sit down. We have a script and process using tablets for each participant to fill our their evaluations during the test. We used 3 different songs, in 2 variant formats, to compare their thoughts on the sound. We also included 2 different styles of headphones to test the music with. We used a specific evaluation format created by Tokyo to gauge the user's interest and feedback using numbered scales, and text entry boxes for qualitative feedback. After conducting multiple sessions, we take the data from the tablets to build data charts to show the results of the test, as well as the qualitative feedback to build a report for the stakeholders.

Setup

Seating for 3, 2 different headphones, 3 songs with 2 variants. Tablets for evaluation and feedback. Moderator guides them through the process and makes sure all evaluations and songs are played in correct order (because the order was mixed half way through the test to eliminate any bias). Sessions were video recorded for data purposes.
Findings
The feature was regarded positively by participants, however most could not confidently say they would use or want the feature without being able to try it out with their own music they are familiar with, and the pricing of the feature was also a concern.
Conclusions and Experience
This was more experience for me moderating tests. This was a bit more systematic than a user test, however it helped me practice talking to users and participants, as well as run a test following a script and using various pieces of technology that I had to know how to use and troubleshoot both hardware and software.
